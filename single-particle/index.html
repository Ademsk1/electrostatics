Single particles

Charged particles emit an Electric field \(E\) through space, which acts on other charged particles. In a scenario where two particles are present, the equation that governs the magnitude of the electric force is very much like the gravitational force: $$F = \frac{kq_1 q_2}{r^2} $$ Field lines are used to represent the electric field, as seen in this model. They are a conventional technique to diagram electrostatic charges. Field lines travel from positive to negative. In this diagram, the particle at the centre is a proton.
